A Study on Cafe Reviews in Roosevelt Neighborhood, Seattle

In this project, the goal is to systematically develop processes for data creation, collection, and formation that captures complex context-rich human spatial experiences. Data for this project will be collected using a wen crawler in Python. To understand the context of data for communicating them in visual form, we will develop strategies to find creative ways of encoding and visualizing spatial data and deliver the context behind them.

Roosevelt Neighborhood and Cafes

Roosevelt is a neighborhood located in Northeast Seattle and is known for its diverse and socially active community that serves as a transportation and commercial hub. If the name of this neighborhood sounded familiar, that is because President Roosevelt grew very fond of this neighborhood and since his death in 1919, the local community remembered his Seattle visits, as well as his effort to preserve much of the neighborhood’s nature and beauty so after a naming contest in 1927, the place was officially named “Roosevelt Neighborhood”. Since then, the neighborhood has maintained its natural beauty. Being able to experience nature while enjoying a nice cup of coffee in the Roosevelt neighborhood has never been easier as the neighborhood boasts more than 15 cafes in that area alone, ranging from franchise cafes to local-owned roasteries. As a coffee enjoyer myself, this made me want to explore the wide range of cafes in the neighborhood and see whether the culture and history here has given their coffee here a unique scent.

Data Collection & Method

During our field scouting of the Roosevelt Neighborhood, we noticed several coffee shops around the area as every corner we made, there would be one. Since Seattle is known as the birthplace of Starbucks and is the city where coffee has turned from a daily necessity to a form of art, we decided to look at coffee shops in Roosevelt Neighborhood and see what past customers had to say in general about the coffee scene here. In this project we used a Yelp web crawler made by Yifan to retrieve several café reviews from Yelp. We then compiled the comments and chose 15 cafes for this project. We then cleaned out the extra reviews and the comments before converting it into a JSON , where we uploaded it to observable to create a radial dendrogram.

Findings and Conclusion

Looking at the radial dendrogram, we can see that most of the comments were positive comments, either complimenting the coffee or the café design, with a few negative comments describing the bad coffee they tasted, or one such comment that said “greedy”. Some of the comments also describe the experience of the café itself. The variance in comments regarding whether they enjoyed the coffee or not could be explained by the wide range of coffee available, such as espressos, cappuccinos, lattes, americanos, or other new types of coffees. Many different factors go into producing the coffee and the resulting taste, from the bean the café uses to the method of brewing and style of coffee. Each person also has their own personal tastes so not everyone will have the same thoughts on the same coffee. This is why although there are various opinions about the cafes as seen on yelp, I would still recommend people going in person to try them out as it may fit their taste better. The comments aside, it was interesting to see the different opinions people had on cafes and made me want to go out to try them all.

Thoughts & Reflection

I personally have only been to Roosevelt Neighborhood once and remember trying one of the coffee shops there. It was a nice and quiet neighborhood to peacefully enjoy some coffee. During the field scouting for this project, while taking photos to create a sketch, I remembered my past experiences and decided to try a different coffee shop. This was when I got the idea of café reviews, as I was also curious to see what people had to say about the coffee in this neighborhood. Me and my group mate’s role were essentially the same for this project as we both went on a scouting trip at our own conveniences, as well as helped each other work on the radial dendrogram. I felt that even if we didn’t get to meet up and discuss together, since we both did our jobs and communicated to each other about what we had to do, we were able to get the assignment done swiftly.


Since the cafes represented in the infographic are ones that we chose and the comments are only the ones from yelp, the dendrogram somewhat represents what people think about cafes in the Roosevelt Neighborhood. The coffee scene is also very big in Seattle which contributed to choosing this topic. Creating the radial dendrogram wasn’t that complicated as the instructions were easy to follow. As for improvements, if we had more time we would have liked to try and gather more comments for each café to give the radial dendrogram more variance.
